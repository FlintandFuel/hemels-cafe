The Experience

Not just a coffee stop. A reason to slow down.

Most cafés are a stop. Hemels is a destination. The kind of place where you arrive for a flat white and find yourself still there an hour later, book open, shoes off on the grass, not entirely sure why you would leave.

Great Coffee

Flat white, cappuccino, iced coffee, chai — made by baristas who actually care. The kind of coffee you order a second before finishing the first. Consistently the first thing guests mention.

Beautiful Garden

Wide lawns. Ancient trees. White walls. The outdoor space is the product — open, uncrowded, and quiet in a way that genuinely surprises people who have only ever driven past on Lynnwood Road.

Community Character

Regular faces. Staff who remember names. A padel court next door, a local gift shop on-site, a car wash on the way out. A place, not just a stop.

Order at the counter. The garden is yours.

Choose your coffee, find your spot in the shade, and let the afternoon take care of itself. No table assignments, no hovering. Just the garden, the coffee, and wherever you want to sit.

What We Serve

Made with care. Worth coming back for.

A focused menu, not minimal for its own sake, but because a shorter list means everything on it is done properly. The cheesecake alone has become a destination order.

Specialty Coffee

Flat white, cappuccino, latte, iced coffee. Made by people who have thought about it. Reliably good — not a given in Pretoria East.

Chai Latte

Properly spiced, warm, and the kind of thing people order twice. Better outside in the garden with sun coming through the trees.

Signature Cheesecake

Multiple reviews mention it unprompted. House-baked, singled out as destination-worthy. Order it. You will understand why.

Swiss Carrot Cake

House-baked, cream-cheese frosted, best alongside a flat white in the garden. On the menu because it earns its place.

Artisan Ciabatta Sandwiches

Sourdough ciabatta with proper fillings: Chorizo, Chicken Mayo, Cheesy Harvest. Guests describe them as exceeding expectations. Good enough to be the reason to visit.

Brownies & Pastries

Belgian chocolate brownies, croissants, muffins — all house-baked. If you came for coffee and left with three pastries, that is the intended outcome.

R100 to R250 per person. Good food, properly made.

The Space

A garden that makes Pretoria feel far away.

On one of the busiest roads in the east — somehow, completely quiet. White architecture, old trees, open lawns. The kind of space you want to show people.

Follow @hemelscafe on Instagram for a real look at what to expect.

Green Grove Precinct

More than coffee. Make a half-day of it.

Hemels sits inside Green Grove — a small precinct on Lynnwood Road with a notable quality: everything here gives you a reason to stay longer than you planned.

Grove Padel

Padel courts directly adjacent to the café. Book a session, play hard, then come straight to Hemels for a post-game coffee and ciabatta. It becomes a Saturday ritual faster than expected.

@grovepadel.pta

Plaaslik

A curated local gift shop inside the precinct. South African-made goods and locally crafted products. The kind of things worth buying, not just picking up. Worth a browse before or after your coffee.

Skhoon Car Wash

Drop your car, order your coffee, and by the time you have finished your cheesecake and an hour in the garden, your car is clean. A better deal than it sounds.
